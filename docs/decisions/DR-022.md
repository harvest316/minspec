# DR-022: Risk-profile ceremony model (consequence axis; screen-gated; tier demoted)

> **Status: proposed — scope-split by [DR-024](DR-024.md).** Accepted in-session,
> then downgraded on review: the new **primary signal (impact-reach) is unvalidated**
> while the diff-size axis earned its place via the DR-009/SPEC-004 study (κ=0.80).
> Only the **Fork B contract _direction_** (profile is the unit; `tier` derived) is
> **accepted** — in DR-024. The reach/consequence analyzers, the risk→phase catalog,
> screen-gated risks **enforcement**, and the **positioning** change are **proposed,
> gated on reach validation ([#91](https://github.com/harvest316/minspec/issues/91))**.
> **[DR-020](DR-020.md) remains in force in the interim** (this DR supersedes it only
> on acceptance). The model below stands as the intended design pending that gate.

## Context

"Just Enough Spec" tiers ceremony (T1–T4) by **diff size** —
[git-analyzer.ts](../../packages/minspec/src/lib/git-analyzer.ts) feeds
[classify()](../../packages/minspec/src/lib/classifier.ts) (max `tierContribution`
across signals); the per-tier phase set lives in `.minspec/config.json`.

Two findings forced a rethink this session:

1. **"Just Enough Spec" conflated two dials** — *consideration* (how thoroughly a
   change is thought through) and *ceremony* (how much the human must read/approve).
   Tiering tied them because the historical cost was *human authoring*. The LLM
   authors now: consideration should be thorough on *every* change (nearly free);
   ceremony should scale with *demonstrated risk*, not diff size.
2. **[DR-021] already proved (κ=0.80, n=120) that diff-size ≠ difficulty** and,
   critically, that the under-tiered misses are **median 1 file / 5 lines** — so
   they are low-*scope* too. DR-021 resolved that fork: ship the classifier as an
   **upward-only ceremony ratchet** (a 100%-precise lower bound), reframe docs to
   "tier = mechanical scope, not difficulty," and defer difficulty detection to an
   **opt-in semantic** layer (near invariant #1, AI-gated).

### The correction DR-021 forces on our reasoning

The session's framing — *"measure the reach, don't enumerate the risks"* — is right
but must be **rescoped**. Reach does **not** catch difficulty (DR-021 proved subtle
fixes are low-scope, hence low-reach). Reach catches a **different, orthogonal
axis**:

- **Consequence / blast radius** — *if this is wrong, how bad?* Call-graph **impact
  reach** (downstream caller count), public-API surface delta, irreversibility,
  sensitive-sink reach. Deterministic, and genuinely new: a 2-line change to a
  200-caller core function is small in *diff* but huge in *reach* — the current
  diff-shape signals miss it.
- **Difficulty / likelihood** — *how likely is this to be wrong?* Orthogonal to both
  size and reach; deterministically invisible (DR-021). Stays the opt-in semantic
  layer DR-021 defined.

Risk = likelihood × consequence. The current classifier sees a crude proxy of
consequence (diff shape) and none of likelihood. This DR adds a **proper
consequence axis** (reach) and routes ceremony off the combined profile, while
**inheriting** DR-021's difficulty-deferred stance unchanged.

### The fork (decided, per the prior session turn)

- **Fork A** — keep scalar `tier` as the unit; only change what drives it.
- **Fork B** — the unit is the **risk profile**; required phases are selected from
  which risks tripped.

A and B fork the **Tier-0 data-model contract** (DR-014): A stores a scalar `tier`
in frontmatter / the approval record / classifier output; B stores a profile.
Choosing A now and B later is a breaking migration of every frontmatter + the
approval store. The decision cannot be deferred without a double re-pour.

## Decision

**Adopt Fork B. The unit of ceremony is a change's _risk profile_ (a consequence
profile in v1), not a diff-size tier. Ceremony = the phases the tripped risks
require. `tier` is demoted to a derived, display-only label. DR-021's ratchet and
difficulty-deferral are inherited, not replaced.**

### 1. The screen runs on every change (always-on consideration)

A **risk screen** evaluates every change — the always-on "think it through" that
"Just Enough Spec" wanted, delivered as a screen, not a mandatory document. Two
layers, mirroring DR-019 / SPEC-012's deterministic/semantic split:

- **Tier-0 deterministic (always, free, offline) — the consequence axis.** Impact
  reach (flagship), public-API surface delta, irreversibility (migrations, deletes,
  schema), sensitive-sink reach (auth/payments/data/secrets), concurrency. Existing
  diff-size signals survive **demoted** — inputs, not the driver. Per DR-021 these
  signals are an **upward-only ratchet**: a tripped signal floors ceremony **up,
  never auto-down**.
- **Tier-1 semantic (opt-in) — the difficulty axis.** Exactly DR-021's deferred
  layer: an AI pass that reads intent for difficulty + non-structural risk (a
  user-facing regex, an upstream rate-limit). AI-consent-gated (invariant #1,
  DR-004), runs at a commit/PR boundary, not every keystroke.

### 2. Risk profile → required phases (ceremony as risk-response)

The screen emits a **risk profile** (tripped signals + severities). A catalog maps
each risk to the phase(s) it demands — sensitive-sink reach → threat-model phase;
irreversibility → migration+rollback phase; public-API delta → contract+consumer
review. Required ceremony = the union. A consequence-heavy *but tiny* change can
require a threat model **without** a full task breakdown — which the T1–T4 ladder
structurally cannot express. The risk→phase **catalog is the real design surface**;
v1 ships a deliberately small one (own spec).

### 3. The Risks & Mitigations section is screen-gated (supersedes DR-020)

The written section is the **human-readable render of the screen**, required **iff
the screen trips**:

- **Screen clean** → no section required; the machine certified low structural
  blast radius. Forcing prose here is the rubber-stamp DR-020 itself warned of (R1).
- **Screen trips** → section required, **scoped to what tripped** ("reaches 14
  callers / touches auth — state the mitigation"). Specific prompt, not a blank page.

Reach replaces the section's *detection* role; the human still owns *mitigation* and
*non-structural* risk. **Screen-clean = "structurally clear," not "safe"** — the
difficulty + semantic gap is the opt-in Tier-1 pass; below it we degrade honestly
(SPEC-010 FR-6 / SPEC-012 FR-15), never pretend.

### 4. `tier` demoted to a derived display label

`tier` is no longer stored or reasoned over. The profile is the source of truth; a
derived "looks like a T3" label MAY show for legibility. Frontmatter carries the
required-phase set / profile; any retained `tier:` is computed, not authored.

### 5. Incremental rollout, contract right from day one

The risk-profile contract lands first (frontmatter never re-poured); the catalog
grows over time. The engine barely changes — `classify()`'s max-over-signals already
implements "escalate to the worst signal" and DR-021's ratchet; we add consequence
analyzers and demote the size signals.

## Costly to Refactor

- **HIGHEST — gated on [#91](https://github.com/harvest316/minspec/issues/91) for exactly this reason.** Demoting `tier` to a derived field of a **risk-profile container** is a **breaking data-model migration once shipped** (Fork B). The container is a superset of a scalar tier, so *choosing* it now is low-regret; *shipping the reach model before #91 validates* is the expensive, hard-to-reverse move. *Check: not shipped ahead of #91; data-model is the Fork-B container.*

## Alternatives considered

- **Fork A — risk-driven but scalar `tier` kept as the unit.** Rejected: bakes the
  wrong unit into the Tier-0 contract; B-later is a breaking migration. Double re-pour.
- **Keep diff-size tiering + blanket/tier-gated risks section (DR-020).** Rejected:
  size ≠ blast radius; blanket prose = rubber-stamped false confidence.
- **Enumerated risk taxonomy as the primary signal.** Rejected as *primary*: a
  checklist catches only foreseeable categories; impact-reach catches the
  structurally-unforeseen consequence. Taxonomy is a secondary signal.
- **Treat reach as the difficulty fix.** Rejected — DR-021's evidence: subtle fixes
  are low-reach. Reach is the *consequence* axis only; difficulty stays opt-in.
- **Defer the A/B decision.** Rejected: A and B fork the data-model contract;
  deferring guarantees a breaking migration. Nothing shipped → now is cheapest.
