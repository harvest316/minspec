# DR-028: Cross-cutting sections complete last; presence never latches "complete"

> **Promoted by [DR-029](DR-029.md) (2026-06-03).** "Completed-last" becomes the
> cross-checks phase authoring sequence; the freshness rule is amended to bind FR-body
> bytes (not ids only). See DR-029 §1, §8.

## Context

Required *cross-cutting* sections — **Risks & Mitigations**, **Consequences** —
summarise the **whole** artifact. A presence check (SPEC-013 FR-1) can verify a
section *exists*; it cannot verify the section still *reflects the current spec*.

The failure mode (raised in the SPEC-013 review session): a Risks section is filled
in early, while the spec is still being built; later FRs are added; the Risks
section now omits them — yet the presence gate still reads ✓. The author trusts it
("that bit's done") and ships a spec whose risks silently under-cover its scope.

This is the **same defect family** this session spent its length killing: a signal
that **latches "complete" once and never re-binds to ground truth** (a `status:`
that said `specifying` while code shipped; an approval that would go stale on edit
were it not hash-bound). Litmus (DR-003): "section is present" is a *symptom* of
completeness; the *mechanism* that should reject a stale-but-present section is
missing.

MinSpec already owns the cure pattern — **[DR-012](DR-012.md)**: approval is
hash-bound, so editing the spec voids the claim and forces re-review. The fix is to
apply that "edit-voids-the-claim" idea to the section-complete claim, and to prevent
the premature claim in the first place.

## Decision

**Cross-cutting required sections are completed last, are due only at the
ready/done transition, and their completeness is freshness- and coverage-bound —
presence alone never marks them complete.**

1. **Completed-last / due-at-ready (prevention).** Cross-cutting sections are
   emitted by scaffolds as **deferred placeholders**, not blank stubs. A deferred
   placeholder is a recognised **third state**, distinct from both *missing* and
   *satisfied*:
   - while `status: specifying` (draft) it is **not "missing"** → no nag, and
   - it is **not "satisfied"** → it can never latch "complete".
   These sections become **due** at the ready/`done` transition (aligns with
   SPEC-013 FR-11 / SPEC-006 RD-2), i.e. after the FRs have stabilised. Early
   authorship is *allowed* (jot a risk mid-thought) — never forced, never forbidden.

2. **Freshness-bound (catch #1, DR-012 pattern).** A completed cross-cutting section
   is bound to a hash of the spec's **FR-set**. Adding/removing/renaming FRs after
   the section was completed marks it **stale**, surfacing a *named* prompt
   ("Risks may not cover FR-7, FR-8, added since the section was last touched —
   revisit"). Binds to FR-set *structure* (ids), not prose edits, to avoid
   false-positives.

3. **Coverage-checked (catch #2, SPEC-010 model).** Each FR should be reflected in
   the cross-cutting section (DR-020 already wants mitigations→FR). Uncovered FRs are
   named. The SPEC-010 coverage DAG is extended to treat required cross-cutting
   sections as covered nodes — tracked as an amendment to the **already-approved**
   SPEC-010 ([#121](https://github.com/harvest316/minspec/issues/121)), not silently
   edited here.

4. **Surfaced by offer, never silent ([DR-026](DR-026.md)).** Staleness / under-coverage
   is advisory during the draft and an offered one-click revisit at the gate — never
   a silent rewrite, never a hard block (only DR-012 approval blocks).

## Consequences

**Positive:**

- **Presence ≠ complete** for cross-cutting sections. The section-complete claim is
  re-bound to the FR-set every time the spec changes — the false-complete window
  closes (closes the same latch-once defect as `status: specifying`-while-shipped,
  cured the DR-012 way: edit-voids-the-claim).
- **Prevention does most of the work.** Because the sections are completed-last
  against stable FRs, the freshness/coverage catches rarely fire — matching the
  session's "ideally the spec is generated properly the first time."
- **Scope stays contained.** Non-cross-cutting required sections (none today) are
  unaffected — they satisfy on presence; the new three-state logic is scoped to the
  Risks/Consequences family only.

**Negative:**

- **SPEC-010 gains a coverage edge** (FR → cross-cutting-section). That amends an
  **already-approved** spec → re-review required, tracked at
  [#121](https://github.com/harvest316/minspec/issues/121); not silently edited here.
  Cost: a re-review round-trip on SPEC-010 before this DR can fully land.
- **SPEC-013 carries new implementation surface** — the deferred-placeholder *third
  state*, the FR-set-hash freshness check, and consumption of the SPEC-010 coverage
  predicate. Cost: three coupled mechanisms to build and test rather than a one-line
  presence check (FR-1).

## Alternatives considered

- **Presence-only (status quo).** Rejected: the exact false-complete this DR exists
  to prevent. (The session: "hoping we can do better than presence-only.")
- **Hard-block authoring the section until FRs are "done".** Rejected: you sometimes
  capture a risk the moment you think of it; blocking that is hostile and
  unenforceable (there is no crisp "FRs done" line mid-draft). Deferred-placeholder +
  due-at-ready gets the benefit without the block.
- **Rely on DR-012 full-spec re-approval only.** Rejected as insufficient *alone*:
  re-approval staleness does block shipping, but it points at "the whole spec", not
  "the Risks section is the stale part" — coarse and late. DR-028 keeps it named and
  early. (DR-012 remains the backstop.)

## Risks & Mitigations

| # | Risk | Likelihood · Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Freshness false-positives** — a cosmetic FR reword marks the section stale, training users to ignore it. | Med · Med | Bind to FR-set *structure* (ids added/removed/renamed), not prose; an FR-ref present in the section clears it. |
| 2 | **Deferred-placeholder confusion** — author reads the placeholder as "done". | Med · Med | Placeholder text is explicit ("⏳ complete after FRs stable — not yet counted"); it never satisfies the predicate; due at the gate. |
| 3 | **Coverage check drifts toward semantic judgement** ("is this risk *good*"). | Low · Med | Coverage is structural (is each FR *referenced*), never a quality oracle — the rule is fixed in SPEC-013 FR-4 ("All checks are structural … never quality"), and SPEC-013 R4 is the standing residual that vacuous-but-specific lines still pass the structural floor. |
| 4 | **Scope spill into SPEC-010** done silently, voiding its approval. | Low · High | SPEC-010 amendment is explicit + tracked (#121) + re-reviewed; not edited as a side effect here. |

## Costly to Refactor

Moderate blast radius — **not** undoable in <1 day once shipped, because the
decision is encoded as data, not just behaviour:

- **Persisted state is the sticky part.** The freshness rule (decision §2) writes an
  **FR-set hash** alongside each completed cross-cutting section, and scaffolds emit a
  **deferred-placeholder third state** (§1) into spec files. Reversing the policy means
  migrating every spec that already stored a section-hash or a placeholder back to the
  binary present/absent model — a data migration across `specs/`, not a code revert.
- **Cross-spec coupling.** The coverage edge lands in **SPEC-010** (the coverage DAG,
  amended via [#121](https://github.com/harvest316/minspec/issues/121)) and the gate
  logic lands in **SPEC-013**; unwinding requires touching two approved specs plus
  re-review, not one file.
- **Cheap parts.** The *advisory* surfacing (§4, DR-026 offer-not-silent) is pure UI and
  could be toggled off in <1 day. The promotion by **[DR-029](DR-029.md)** (FR-body-byte
  binding) tightened §2 further, deepening this coupling rather than loosening it.

ADR-filter: **No** — cannot be cleanly undone in <1 day (persisted hashes/placeholders +
two-spec coupling), which is why it is a recorded DR rather than a just-do-it change.
