# DR-026: Detect-and-offer, never silent — for missing required sections

> **Amended by [DR-029](DR-029.md) (2026-06-03).** "Stub, not content" is carved out
> for the Zone B self-audit appendix only: in the cross-checks phase the LLM may
> author section *content* (offer-never-silent + confirm-before-write postures kept).
> Zone A stays fully under this DR. See DR-029 §6.

## Context

[SPEC-013](../../specs/minspec/SPEC-013-risk-section-policy/requirements.md) enforces
*required sections* (Risks & Mitigations; Consequences). The session asked for the
gap to be closed **seamlessly, without the nag** — ideally the section is just
present, and when it is not, MinSpec "just adds it" rather than asking
"oops, want me to add it?".

Two truths pulled against each other:

1. **Prevention is the real goal.** A correctly *generated* artifact never misses a
   section. The template/scaffold path (SPEC-013 FR-3) is the primary mechanism; the
   detect/fix path should rarely fire at all.
2. **Silent auto-write collides with an established, already-shipped posture.** Two
   **approved** specs make non-silent a load-bearing invariant:
   - [SPEC-005](../../specs/minspec/SPEC-005-auto-structure-repair/requirements.md) —
     structure repair is **offer-never-silent** (its FR-3).
   - [SPEC-010](../../specs/minspec/SPEC-010-signpost-correctness/requirements.md) — the
     signpost is **advisory**; it never writes the developer's artifacts (its FR-5 /
     INV-advisory).
   Silently writing a stub — even an empty one — would overturn that posture across
   three specs at once.

The deciding insight came from the UX angle: **a visible detect-and-fix is itself
the product demonstrating its value.** A silent fix is invisible — the developer
never sees the guardrail work, so the guardrail earns no trust. Seeing MinSpec
*catch* a missing section and *offer the one-click fix* is a feature, not friction.
That removes the apparent conflict: the anti-nag ask is satisfied by a single
actionable offer (not a repeated passive warning), while offer-never-silent holds.

## Decision

**A missing required section is surfaced as a visible, one-click offer/quick-fix —
never silently written, never a bare passive nag.**

- **Prevent first.** Scaffolds emit the section stubs (SPEC-013 FR-3); a properly
  generated artifact never reaches the detect path. This is the ideal state.
- **Offer, don't silently write.** When a section is nonetheless missing, MinSpec
  emits a diagnostic that carries a **one-click "Add section" action** which inserts
  the (empty/deterministic) stub on the developer's confirmation. This reuses
  SPEC-005's offer-never-silent contract; SPEC-010's signpost stays read-only.
- **Stub, not content.** The action inserts only the structural slot (heading +
  skeleton). The section's actual *content* (the risks/consequences) is always
  author-written — never fabricated.
- **Dirty-editor safe.** The fix applies as a confirmable `WorkspaceEdit` and never
  clobbers unsaved changes (mirrors SPEC-010 FR-14, SPEC-005 non-destructive INV).

The rejected alternative — a **silent** structural-stub insert — was considered and
declined: the consistency cost (overturning offer-never-silent across SPEC-005/010)
and the lost trust signal (an invisible fix demonstrates nothing) both outweigh the
marginal seamlessness over a single click.

## Consequences

**Positive:**

- SPEC-013's enforcement becomes **prevent + offer-to-fix**, not warn-only and not
  silent-write. Its INV-advisory is restated as *"warns and offers a one-click fix;
  never silently writes; never blocks."*
- **No posture change to the approved specs.** SPEC-005 (offer-never-silent, its
  FR-3) and SPEC-010 (advisory, read-only, its FR-5/INV-advisory) stand unaltered;
  this DR confirms and extends their shared rule to the required-section case rather
  than carving an exception — one fewer special-case to reason about.
- The detect→offer→fix loop is an intentional, **visible** trust signal — surfaced
  via the validator channel (CLI v1) and the Problems panel
  ([#118](https://github.com/harvest316/minspec/issues/118)) — so the guardrail
  earns trust by being seen working (the deciding UX insight from Context).
- The fix applies as a confirmable, non-destructive `WorkspaceEdit` (Decision
  "Dirty-editor safe"; mirrors SPEC-010 FR-14, SPEC-005 non-destructive INV), so
  enabling it cannot clobber unsaved author edits.

**Negative:**

- Because content is never auto-written (Decision "Stub, not content"), an
  inserted-but-empty stub still leaves work on the author: it warns again at the
  ready/`done` transition (SPEC-006 RD-2 alignment) — the gap is surfaced twice, not
  closed by the click. This is the deliberate cost of refusing to fabricate
  risks/consequences content (DR-020).
- "Prevent first" relies on the scaffold path (SPEC-013 FR-3) staying correct; every
  artifact that bypasses the scaffold still hits the detect/offer path, so the
  offer-fatigue risk (Risk #1) is never fully eliminated, only made rare.

## Alternatives considered

- **Silent structural-stub insert (no nag, no click).** The session's first
  framing. Rejected: overturns offer-never-silent across two approved specs, and a
  silent fix is invisible — it forfeits the "watch MinSpec catch it" trust signal
  that makes the detection valuable.
- **Bare passive warning only (the status quo of early SPEC-013 drafts).** Rejected
  as a *dead nag*: "oops, missing a bit" with no one-click remedy is exactly the
  friction the session wanted gone.
- **Auto-fill content (e.g. write "None material" at T1 automatically).** Rejected:
  crosses from structure into authoring judgement; the written-thought is the whole
  point of the section (DR-020), so the author must write it.

## Risks & Mitigations

| # | Risk | Likelihood · Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Offer fatigue** on a tree with many missing sections (e.g. 25 legacy DRs) re-creates the nag it replaced. | Med · Med | Prevention-first + backfill-before-enable (SPEC-013 RD-1); offer is one-click and dismissible per artifact (SPEC-010 FR-7 override memory). |
| 2 | **Scope drift toward silent writes** later "for convenience", eroding the posture. | Low · High | This DR makes offer-never-silent explicit for required sections; any future silent-write needs a superseding DR, not a quiet code change. |
| 3 | **Empty-stub gaming** — author clicks "add", leaves the stub blank, ships. | Med · Low | Inserted stub still fails the content predicate; warns at the `done`/gate transition (SPEC-006 RD-2). Advisory-only, caught at DR-012 review. |

## Costly to Refactor

**Mostly cheap to reverse — one load-bearing exception.** The detect→offer→fix
mechanism itself is a thin diagnostic + quick-fix in the validator/Problems-panel
channel (#118); swapping the *posture* of this single feature (e.g. to warn-only)
touches only that code path and is well under a day.

What is expensive is the **invariant this DR ratifies**, not the feature. Once
offer-never-silent is declared the shared rule across SPEC-005 (FR-3), SPEC-010
(FR-5/INV-advisory) and now SPEC-013, reversing it to allow *silent writes* is a
multi-spec change: it would re-open all three postures, contradict the Risk #2
mitigation ("any future silent-write needs a superseding DR, not a quiet code
change"), and require a superseding DR plus re-review of every artifact-touching
path. DR-029's later carve-out (Zone B self-audit may author *content*) shows the
blast radius — even a scoped exception needed its own DR and an explicit amendment
banner on this one (lines 14-17).

ADR-filter: the *feature wiring* — yes, undoable in <1 day. The *offer-never-silent
invariant* — no; that is the durable commitment and is intentionally costly to undo.
